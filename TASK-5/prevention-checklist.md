# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online — Checklist for College Students in India

> **Your digital safety is in your hands.** Follow these steps to protect yourself from cybercrime, scams, and online threats.

---

## ✅ General Cybersecurity Tips

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
  Create passwords with a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols (e.g., `Tr!angle@92`). Never reuse the same password across platforms like Instagram, Gmail, and your college portal.

- [ ] **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
  Turn on 2FA for Gmail, Instagram, WhatsApp, and your banking apps. This ensures that even if someone steals your password, they cannot log in without a second verification step.

- [ ] **Never click on unknown links in SMS, WhatsApp, or email.**
  Scammers often send fake scholarship offers, job alerts, or "KYC update required" messages with malicious links. Always verify the sender before clicking anything.

- [ ] **Keep your phone and apps updated.**
  Software updates patch security vulnerabilities. Enable automatic updates for your OS and apps — especially banking and UPI apps — to stay protected against known exploits.

- [ ] **Use only secure, trusted Wi-Fi networks.**
  Avoid using public Wi-Fi at cafes, bus stops, or college canteens for logging into banking, email, or social media. If you must, use a reputable VPN to encrypt your connection.

- [ ] **Review app permissions regularly.**
  Check which apps have access to your camera, microphone, contacts, and location. Revoke permissions that aren't necessary for the app to function — especially for apps downloaded from outside the Play Store/App Store.

- [ ] **Be cautious of fake job and internship offers.**
  If a recruiter asks you to pay a "registration fee" or "security deposit" for a job or internship, it is almost certainly a scam. Legitimate employers never ask candidates for money upfront.

- [ ] **Protect your social media privacy settings.**
  Set your Instagram, Snapchat, and Facebook profiles to private. Avoid publicly posting your phone number, home address, college ID, or daily schedule — this information can be used to target or impersonate you.

## 💳 Financial & UPI Safety Tips

- [ ] **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank account details with anyone — ever.**
  No bank, government officer, or customer support agent will ever ask for your UPI PIN or OTP over a call or message. If someone does, hang up immediately — it is a fraud attempt.

- [ ] **Understand the difference between "Request Money" and "Send Money" on UPI apps.**
  Scammers trick victims into approving a money *request* by claiming it will "send" them prize money or a refund. On PhonePe, GPay, or Paytm — you only enter your PIN when **sending** money, not receiving it. If you're asked to enter your PIN to "receive" funds, stop — you are being scammed.

- [ ] **Verify UPI IDs and phone numbers before every transaction.**
  Before sending money, double-check the recipient's name that appears after entering their UPI ID or number. Scammers create UPI IDs that look similar to legitimate ones (e.g., `support.paytm@icici` vs. the real address).

- [ ] **Set a daily UPI transaction limit in your banking app.**
  Most banks allow you to cap your daily UPI transfer limit. Set it to a reasonable amount so that even if your account is compromised, the damage is limited.

## 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India

If you are a victim of any online fraud, scam, phishing, or cybercrime, report it immediately:

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File a complaint online 24/7 |
| 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — Call to report financial fraud and get immediate assistance |
| 🏛️ **Local Cyber Cell** | Visit your nearest police station's cyber cell for in-person assistance |
| 📧 **Report Phishing Emails** | Forward suspicious emails to `report@phishing.gov.in` |

> ⚡ **Act fast!** For financial fraud, call **1930** within the first few hours. Early reporting significantly increases the chances of freezing the fraudulent transaction and recovering your money.
